Spent the morning iterating on the dashboard layout — specifically the tension between information density and scannability. The current design tries to show too much at once. Users are reporting they don't know where to look.

Pulled apart the card grid and started with a single-column feed instead. Counterintuitive given the wide canvas, but it forced a clearer reading order. Everything has a hierarchy now: primary metric at top, trend line below, then supporting detail.

![Dashboard wireframe exploration](/images/wireframes.svg)

The biggest shift was moving filters out of the top nav and into a persistent left rail. This mirrors how people actually refine data — iteratively, not as a pre-step. Tested with three internal users this afternoon; all three found the filter without being prompted.

**Open questions:**

- How does the left rail behave on narrow viewports?
- Should filters persist across sessions or reset on close?
- The trend line needs a zero baseline — current version is misleading at small scales.

Next: prototype the mobile layout and figure out the filter persistence model.
